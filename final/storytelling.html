The storytelling aspect of this game is one of the best parts about it, and in the role of the DM, you get to have one of, if not the most fun part all to yourself.

WORLDBUILDING!!!

With this power in mind, please don't be cringe. Don't try to force a moment to be emotional. It has never worked, and it never will. If you try to force them to care about something, they will feel like it is your moment, not everyone's. Players should be the only one's having moments. You're moments are everyone's moments. If you want them to feel invested, you need to make it about something they care about.

There are a million ways to make your players feel connected to the story. But the good news is, you don't have to guess which one is best. You can talk with your players, asking them what they want out of their character's story. DO THIS!! They don't have to dictate every circumstance that their players will be in, but knowing the kind of narrative they want out of their character will help them look back at the story you helped them tell with joy, rather than regret or feeling like it was a waste.

Clutching onto the game so hard you crush the players inside is the most surefire way to tank a campaign. You don't have to come up with your own magic system to make an interesting world, and you don't have to make your players fight for scraps of success to get them to celebrate their victories. Don't try to turn it into a contest between you and your party. You are all on the same team, trying to tell the story together.
