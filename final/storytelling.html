The storytelling aspect of this game is one of the best parts about it, and in the role of the DM, you get to have one of, if not the most fun part all to yourself.

WORLDBUILDING!!!

As the Dungeon master, you get to pick what setting the story takes place in, what is at stake, what options are given to your players, and ultimately whether they fail or succeed. You are running the show... so make sure they call the shots.

Your players need to feel like what they are doing matters. I they need to feel like heroes. Or villans. They need to be in control unless there is something specifically pushing back on them in one specific area. Too often, I see DM's feel the need to put their players in their place. As if they are scared that they won't be respected, or their party won't have fun.

But clutching onto the game so hard you crush the players inside is the most surefire way to tank a campaign. You don't have to come up with your own magic system to make an interesting world, and you don't have to make your players fight for scraps of success to get them to celebrate their victories. Don't try to turn it into a contest between you and your party. You are all on the same team, trying to tell the story together. This isn't your story. This is their story.
